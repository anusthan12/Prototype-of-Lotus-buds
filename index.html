A World of Equal Opportunities for Every Child

Our vision is a world where every child can thrive, no matter their socio-economic background.

In Boulak al-Dakrour in Cairo, Egypt, Lotus Buds is empowering underprivileged children by providing access to quality education, healthcare services, and community empowerment programs, laying the foundation for a brighter, more equitable future for the children.

We believe every child deserves the chance to thrive. Together, with partners on the ground, we are breaking down the barriers that hold children back, so that regardless of their socio-economic background, they have the support and opportunities they need to reach their full potential.

Our Mission

Lotus Buds' Mission is to empower underprivileged children by ensuring equitable access to quality education, healthcare, and community engagement, breaking cycles of poverty and creating opportunities for every child to thrive.

Our Vision

We envision a world where every child, regardless of background, has the opportunities, support, and resources needed to realize their full potential and lead a healthy, fulfilled life.

Founder's Story

Wafaa Jorgensen is the founder, President, and current CEO of Lotus Buds. Born and raised in Boulak al Dakrour, she received her early education locally in Cairo before earning a Master degree in International Marketing & Management from CBS university in Denmark.

Earlier in 1998, she left Egypt and went on to experience life in developed nations with accessible higher education and abundant job opportunities, which gave her a new perspective. On visits back to Egypt, especially to her childhood neighbourhood, she was struck by the deep contrast in living conditions, particularly for children.

There was a need to develop a physical location where children could come to, where they can feel safe, learn and eat a healthy meal, and enjoy their childhood. The Lotus Buds center was created, opened in May 2023, as a place where children can come to learn, and discuss important matters like basic literacy, e.g. reading, writing, and math, along with the history and culture of Egypt, and the big world beyond their own borders.

Wafaa credits her parents, and especially her father who had an open mind and saw the unique abilities and potentials in each of his three daughters, for inspiring the work she does today and her deep-rooted values and principles, starting with the importance of education.

About the Lotus Buds Center

The Lotus Buds Center, located in Boulak al-Dakrour in Cairo, creates an enriching environment that fosters mutual respect, allowing underprivileged Egyptian children to gather, learn and engage in a safe manner.

Through the dedication of volunteers, we provide these children with valuable opportunities for education, vocational training, and healthcare in terms of getting them into healthier nutritional habits, while also addressing their psychological well-being.

The Center is housed in the basement of a church in Boulak al-Dakrour in Cairo, Egypt. The church donated the dilapidated space for free, and through donations, will-power and energy, funds were raised to renovate the space to make it safe, clean and functional.

Facilities Include:
  • Filtered cold water
  • Clean restrooms
  • Several classrooms
  • Internet and computers
  • Kitchen for healthy meals

Our Children

Lotus Buds serves children aged 5-15 years old in the Boulak al-Dakrour neighborhood. The program is open to anyone and people either self-identify as having the need for the program, or are recommended by someone in the community.

We provide a nurturing space where children can access nutritious meals, engage in recreational activities, and receive support with their studies, fostering a sense of belonging and community.
